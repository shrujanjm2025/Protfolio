Issac Newton

Born: 4 January 1643 | Died: 31 March 1727

Biography

Isaac Newton was an English physicist, mathematician, and one of the most influential scientific figures of the Scientific Revolution. He is best known for his three laws of motion, his law of universal gravitation, and his invention of calculus.

Isaac Newton changed the way we understand the Universe. Revered in his own lifetime, he discovered the laws of gravity and motion and invented calculus. He helped to shape our rational world view.

Isaac Newton's most famous equation is F = ma, which represents his second law of motion.

Key Contributions
  • Calculus
  • Universal Gravitation
  • Optics
